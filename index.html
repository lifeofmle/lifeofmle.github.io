Project Details

    Transforming business

    Analysis, Wireframes, Interviews, Coding
    Date : March 2012 - February 2014
    Industry : Investment Banking
    Categories : Analysis, Wireframes, Interviews, Coding

    The Challenge

    The business was using spreadsheets to price bonds. This process was prone to delay of information in the event of someone forgetting to send an email with the updated spreadsheet. We were tasked to analyse the process, calculation and recommend and implement a solution that would improve the business.

    The Solution

    The final solution was a client/server application which allowed users to price bonds. This fulfilled the ability to have the process centralized and gave extra audit points around the process. I was responsible for creating and coding the UI using C# and WPF. The style was based on a flat theme.

    The Result

    The initial pilot program proved to be useful for a large set of the full population of users. Work is still ongoing to improve the system and implement features such as reporting.

    Envisioning the Future

    Sketching, Visual Design
    Date : July 2013
    Industry : Investment Banking
    Categories : Sketching, Visual Design

    The Challenge

    The client was looking to update their logo to reflect the new technologies that would be used in the new system. They wanted something that had a nice visual impact for the end users.

    Initial ideas ranged from different people voicing different desires. Using that as a base, I created some quick logos and showed them around to the team. General feedback was used to pick the winner from the pack.

    One of the favourite design was to have the ICE cube logo to look like a shield. However with the lack of brand colour and feel of a financial system this design was scraped.

    The Result

    The final design is shown below with the red background. This fits more inline with the client's brand colours. The mountains are a functional design additive as it provides visual appeal but also simulates charts and graphs that appear in the system.

    Asking the right Questions

    Analysis, Interviews
    Date : June 2012 - August 2012
    Industry : Human Resources
    Categories : Analysis, Interviews

    The Challenge

    There was a perception of low employee engagement within our company. Different strategies were being implemented and pushed by management but they didn't seem to be working well. What could be done to improve this environment?

    The Solution

    I teamed up with a colleague to help investigate and analyze the issue of with employee satisfaction. Instead of guessing about what the problem was, we decided to conduct interviews employees to find out how they were feeling. To come up with the questions we each came up with questions we thought were important then we grouped them together using card sorting.

    The Result

    We interviewed 20 consultants on our own time before work or over our lunch break. The results of the survey lead to some changes in training initiatives and helped upper management understand the problem better.

    Simplifying Ideas

    Sketching, Wireframes, Prototyping, Coding
    Date : November 2012
    Industry : Mobile
    Categories : Sketching, Wireframes, Prototyping, Coding
    Website : Proof of Concept

    The Challenge

    Finding new things to do or chaining events together for a perfect birthday or date is hard if you have to travel around to much. You have to search multiple websites to get ideas for dinner and another for things to do. Can there be only one place to go?

    The Solution

    Planbow was my idea to create a service that aggregates Foursquare data to prove you with the best options for your activities. This was a proof of concept to see how Azure websites work and how to integrate with MapBox and Foursquare APIs by using KnockoutJS.

    The Result

    I created this map of my recently visisted restaurants back in 2012. It was well received by friends but gathering content for the site turned out to be a difficult task to sustain to build more material.

    Presenting Ideas

    Presentations
    Date : November 2012
    Industry : Social Media
    Categories : Presentations

    The Challenge

    To build my skills in public speaking, I volunteered to be part of the Ignite London #7 event in Shoreditch, London to speak about “Why we take pictures of food”. This talk was based on the Ignite presentation method of 20 auto-rotating slides in 5 minutes.

    The Solution

    With slides changing automatically every 20 seconds, you have to have a clear concept and flow to your delivery. Using chunking methods I was able to group and organize the information in an entertaining way for telling a story
